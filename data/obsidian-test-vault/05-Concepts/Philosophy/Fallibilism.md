# Fallibilism

The philosophical doctrine that absolute certainty about knowledge is impossible.

## Core Tenets

1. **All knowledge is conjectural** — theories are guesses, not derived from observations
2. **Error is inevitable** — we can be wrong about anything
3. **Criticism is the engine of progress** — through refutation we improve

## Popper's Formulation

[[Karl Popper]] argued that science progresses not by verification but by **falsification**:

> "No number of white swans can prove all swans are white. One black swan can disprove it."

## Deutsch's Extension

[[David Deutsch]] in [[The Beginning of Infinity]]:

- Problems are inevitable
- Problems are soluble
- Explanations can always be improved

## Implications for PKM

Your notes are never "finished." They are:
- Conjectures about what matters
- Subject to revision
- Improved by linking and criticism
