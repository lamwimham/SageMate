High-quality protein refers to dietary protein sources evaluated not just by total content, but by digestibility and essential amino acid profile. Quality is standardized using the Digestible Indispensable Amino Acid Score (DIAAS), a metric developed by the FAO to measure how well the body can absorb and utilize amino acids.

## DIAAS Thresholds
- **Qualified**: DIAAS > 75
- **High-Quality/Excellent**: DIAAS > 100

## Key Metrics & Selection Criteria
Protein selection must account for bioavailability, not just gram weight. Higher DIAAS scores correlate with better muscle protein synthesis, greater thermic effect of food, and improved satiety during caloric restriction.
