MCP servers are backend components that expose data sources, APIs, and executable tools to AI applications using the Model Context Protocol. By implementing an MCP server, developers grant AI models standardized, secure access to external resources such as calendars, databases, design platforms, and hardware interfaces.

## Role in the Architecture
Servers act as the bridge between static data/tools and dynamic AI agents. They translate external capabilities into structured formats that AI clients can understand, query, and invoke, enabling agents to retrieve real-time context and execute complex workflows on behalf of users.
