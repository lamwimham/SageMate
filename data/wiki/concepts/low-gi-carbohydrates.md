Low-GI (Glycemic Index) carbohydrates are carbohydrate sources that cause a slower, more gradual rise in blood sugar levels. By minimizing insulin spikes, they reduce insulin resistance and fat storage risks while prolonging satiety and improving metabolic efficiency for fat burning.

## GI Thresholds
- **Qualified**: GI <= 70
- **Excellent**: GI <= 55
- **Recommendation**: Prioritize foods at the excellent threshold for optimal anti-glycemic effects, balancing texture and taste.

## Physiological Mechanisms
- Slows digestive transit and nutrient absorption
- Moderates insulin response, lowering fat storage signals
- Enhances satiety, naturally reducing total daily caloric intake
- Improves metabolic flexibility, supporting fat oxidation
