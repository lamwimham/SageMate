Dietary fiber consists of non-digestible plant carbohydrates that physically slow digestion, delay nutrient absorption, and act as prebiotics for the gut microbiome. Adequate fiber intake is critical for prolonging fullness, stabilizing blood sugar, and maintaining gastrointestinal regularity.

## Selection Threshold
- **Minimum Recommended**: > 2.5g fiber per 100g of vegetable

## Physiological Benefits
- **Satiety Extension**: Slows gastric emptying, reducing overall calorie consumption
- **Gut Health**: Feeds beneficial intestinal bacteria, improving microbiome diversity
- **Digestive Regularity**: Prevents constipation and promotes consistent bowel function
- **Metabolic Support**: Attenuates postprandial glucose spikes
